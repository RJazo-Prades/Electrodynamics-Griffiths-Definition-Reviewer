Electric Charge Properties

Definition

Electric charge is a fundamental property of matter:

  • Comes in two kinds (positive and negative)
  • Is conserved (cannot be created or destroyed)
  • Is quantized (exists in discrete units)

1. Two Types of Charge

Charges can neutralize each other:

\( (+q) + (-q) = 0 \)

If imbalance is as small as 1 part in \(10^{10}\), matter would explode.

2. Conservation of Charge

Total charge of the universe is constant:

\( Q_{\text{total}} = \text{constant} \)

Charge moves continuously. Expressed by the continuity equation:

\[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{J} + \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} = 0 \]

where \( \rho \) = charge density, \( \mathbf{J} \) = current density.

3. Quantization of Charge

Charge exists in integer multiples of the elementary charge \(e\):

\( q = n \cdot e, \quad n \in \mathbb{Z} \)

Fundamental charge: \( e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \,\text{C} \)

Examples:

  • Proton: \( q = +e \)
  • Electron: \( q = -e \)
  • Carbon nucleus: \( q = +6e \)

Summary

  • Two kinds: \( +q \) and \( -q \), cancel when combined.
  • Conserved: \( Q_{\text{total}} = \text{constant}, \quad \nabla \cdot \mathbf{J} + \dfrac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} = 0 \).
  • Quantized: \( q = n \cdot e, \quad e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \,\text{C} \).
